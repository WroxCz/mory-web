Chapter X. - The warning
Suddenly, from the dark corners of the old, ruined temple, a figure emerged — a middle-aged woman with light brown hair threaded with a few silver strands, tied neatly into a smooth bun. Her light green eyes shone with wisdom and calm.
“Welcome,” she said, her voice firm. “Congratulations on completing the trial.”
The woman slowly stepped closer, her gaze fixed on all three of them.
“I am Lysara,” she introduced herself quietly, yet with unwavering authority in her voice. “A healer from the Tower of Pure Light, who nearly five hundred years ago helped seal Morr'kalil — a being whose darkness almost consumed our world.”
Her words carried a weight that echoed through the temple like the memory of ancient times.
“I created the Fragment of Time you have just passed through. This fragment was meant to serve as a warning. The beginning of a new era is coming — a time when you must be stronger, wiser, and more united than ever before. Each of us has a role in this game of fate. Your journey has only just begun.”
The silence that followed was not empty, but filled with anticipation and resolve.
Aaliyah knew that although difficult days awaited her, she was no longer alone. With James and Rowena by her side, she had the strength to begin again — this time with a clear purpose and a heart open to true love and courage.
Lysara looked at them more seriously now. Her green eyes darkened with concern hidden deep beneath her calm.
“I must warn you,” she began slowly. “Morr'kalil’s bonds are weakening. His darkness longs to return, and if that happens, it will mean devastation for the entire world.”
From a small chest, she took out a tiny orb that glowed with a soft, muted light and handed it to Rowena.
“This is a sealing orb,” she explained. “It contains a portion of the magical power that keeps Morr'kalil bound. You must protect it, find the other three, and in the end use them all to seal Morr'kalil again, should his darkness attempt to break free.”
Aaliyah felt the weight of responsibility Lysara had placed upon them.
“Unfortunately, the time I have is running out. I cannot remain here any longer, nor can I provide you with more information. You must rely on yourselves — your strength and your courage are now your only weapons against the darkness that is approaching.”
Her voice softened into a whisper, as if she were saying farewell to the time she had left.
“Remember that you are not alone. The light can shine again, if you stand firm and act together.”
With one final look filled with wisdom and quiet blessing, Lysara vanished as silently as she had appeared — leaving them with a sense of new responsibility.

The temple began to tremble, as if time itself had realized its purpose had been fulfilled.
The walls, once still, shuddered in a quiet pulse of magical energy.
The light in the corners of the darkened halls began to fade. The silver runes carved into the stone slowly disappeared.
The Fragment of Time — Lysara’s final creation — was collapsing.
Aaliyah instinctively pressed herself against James, seeking comfort and certainty in the crumbling world around them.
His arm immediately wrapped around her with a firmness that reminded her she was not alone.
Without hesitation, he extended his other arm and pulled Rowena close as well.
He drew both women into his embrace, as if he wanted to shield them from everything that was coming — from darkness, from loss, even from the very end of time.
Rowena stiffened for a moment, surprised by the gesture.
Then she relaxed, feeling the quiet strength and warmth of shared closeness.
The three of them, bound together in that embrace, faced the collapsing reality with silent determination.
And then…everything vanished.
