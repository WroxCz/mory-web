Chapter XXXI. - The voice in the stone
One night, when the castle lights dimmed and the bells announced midnight, Brienne slipped into a corridor where no one stood guard. Her quiet steps led through dark passageways where stone walls swallowed sound and the cold bit into her skin like sharp claws.
She stopped at a door at the end of one of the side corridors — old, nearly forgotten, covered in cobwebs and grime, as if the castle itself had pushed it from memory.
She grasped the rusted ring, and the door opened with effort. The stairs beyond descended deep beneath the fortress, into forgotten tunnels.
She moved downward slowly.
The air was heavy. Dead. As if time itself did not exist here. Only silence. And something that breathed in the dark.
She stopped at one of the arched alcoves.
The stones were black, coated with soot and ash. On the floor was carved a symbol — an eye without a lens, surrounded by runes that glowed faintly.
Brienne fell to her knees.
Then, in the corner, a massive shadow appeared — so dense and black it could almost be felt. It gathered into a single form.
From the stone stepped Zarveth.
Without a sound. Without breath. He was there. As if he had always been.
His face was black, but his eyes — empty and soulless — shone like molten gold. In his hands, a sword of black iron, and on his back, a dark spear.
“Good work,” he said in a quiet but cutting voice. “They cling to you as if you were their salvation. And yet they have no idea that you are their ruin.”
Brienne lowered her gaze, but a flicker of cold flashed in her eyes.
Zarveth stepped closer. He reached out and touched her chin. His fingers were like stone — cold, hard, lifeless.
“I must learn more. The seal is nearing weakness. Someone is not holding it firmly enough. And when it falls…”
His voice darkened. “When it falls, our lord Morr'kalil will rise. And the world will tear apart.”
Brienne nodded. “The castle is open. Parsons and Hander are blinded by their own pride. They believe they have everything under control. DaVinci can do nothing — they will not allow him to.”
Zarveth smiled — a flash of dark satisfaction.
“Access to the water is guarded, but the temple is not sealed. The mages hold the keys. Some are tired. Someone may be disappointed. Perhaps… ready to hear another voice.”
“Find me someone who can be broken. Someone who will open the path. And be ready — soon I will need you for more than whispers.”
He turned — shadows folding behind him like veils of darkness.
Then he paused for one final moment.
“And Brienne…”
The girl looked up.
“When they touch you… when they offer you friendship… accept it. And then kill them.”
And then he was gone.
As if the stone itself had swallowed him.
Brienne remained kneeling in silence for a while.
Then she slowly rose.
The air around her grew colder, but she felt nothing.
Neither guilt.
Nor pride.
Only quiet obedience.
And patience.
There were only shadows. Their existence belonged to their master. And Stonegard Castle was already infected.
No one knew it yet.
