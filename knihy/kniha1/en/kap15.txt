Chapter XV. - Whispering currents and a shadow in the sand
Rowena woke suddenly, as if something had called her.
Silence filled the tent.
The orb inside her bag glowed faintly, as if it too sensed the call. Slowly, she reached for it and took it into her palm. Its light brightened slightly, as though responding to her touch.
Carefully, she rose, draped her cloak over her shoulders, and slipped past the sleeping Aaliyah. The tent fabric rustled softly behind her.
The voice came again — closer now, clearer. It led her farther, beyond the fig trees. Toward the old sanctuary with the spring. Rowena approached, her heart beating in a strange rhythm. The orb grew warm, shining with a golden-white glow. She stepped inside. In the cool stone air, she felt something like breath — a gentle whisper of wind brushing against her neck. At the center of the sanctuary was the spring. Crystal-clear water swirled calmly within a small stone basin.
Rowena knelt beside it, placed her hand on the edge, and lowered her head.
And then the water spoke.
“I remember you, Child of Light,” came a voice that did not sound aloud, but formed directly within her mind.
She remained calm.
“Why did you call me?” she whispered.
“I do not call. I answer. You asked — even without words. And I know what you seek.”
“Morr'kalil,” she spoke the name again — the name that had lodged like a thorn in her dreams.
“His name is older than the sand of this land. His hunger endures.” The water rippled, revealing images: ancient gates, corrupted temple walls, figures in black cloaks, pupil-less eyes staring from the depths, and a silent call echoing across the ages.
“Where must we go?” Rowena asked, and her orb shone brighter.
“To the place where stone drank blood and swords raised walls. To the place, where human pride stood against gods. To the place, where the first seal trembled.”
The voice faded, but the ripples in the water formed a pattern — a map.
Not complete. But a guide.
Rowena leaned closer to the surface, her eyes tracing the shifting lines in the water.
“That is the castle… Stonegard,” she said quietly, her voice filled with certainty rising from deep within her instinct.
Rowena slowly stood. She hid the orb beneath her cloak and returned to the tent. In her eyes reflected a quiet resolve.
She knew where they had to go.
