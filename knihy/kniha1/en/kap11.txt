Chapter XI. - The return
Eyes opened into the harsh sunlight.
Aaliyah squinted as she realized she could feel scorching sand beneath her feet. The air was dry, burning, and heavy — so different from the cold, silent temple where the last trace of Lysara’s magic had just vanished.
They were back.
In the middle of the desert.
James was still holding both women in his arms.
For a moment, they remained exactly as the Fragment of Time had left them, until they were certain the desert around them was not another illusion.
Rowena was the first to carefully step away.
She looked around — the dunes surrounding them appeared the same as when they had entered the temple — but something was different.
The air felt heavier.
Not calm.
Tense.
“We’re back,” she stated curtly.
Aaliyah knelt down and grasped a handful of sand, letting it slip slowly through her fingers.
It was warm.
Burning.
Real.
“It almost feels like nothing has changed,” she said quietly.
But when she looked at the glowing orb in Rowena’s hand, she knew everything was different.
James swept his gaze across the horizon — the shadows of dunes and the sunlight reflecting off the scorching ground.
“I don’t know how much time has passed,” he muttered. “But if Morr'kalil is weakening his bonds, we need to act quickly.”
Rowena nodded. “We need to find the nearest shelter. And a map. Then we must determine where the seals are located.”
Aaliyah stood, her hands clenched into fists.
Her heart was beating faster than before — not from fear, but from the realization that she was no longer a girl who merely followed others.
She was part of something greater.
James gave them a short nod. “We’ll head east. I remember the Sanctuary Oasis is about a day’s journey from here.” A brief pause. “If we survive the thirst.”
They set off across the dunes.
Hot air shimmered above the ridges of sand, and their shadows stretched into the distance.
They walked together — three travelers in the wilderness, carrying light in the form of a small orb that might decide the fate of the entire world.
