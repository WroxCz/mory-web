Chapter II. - The sands of the Moonlight Desert
Several weeks had passed since they left the ruins of Turayne, and their journey had eventually led them into the harsh expanse of the Moonlight Desert. The sun hung in the sky like a blazing disc. The sand beneath their feet cracked and burned. Though the land appeared empty, every rumble of wind concealed movement — snakes, sand thieves, and enormous sand worms.
Rowena sweated beneath her dark blue robe, whose fabric had long since lost its sheen. The water in her waterskin was running low. Her magic shielded her from the heat, but even magic did not possess endless reserves. Still, she said nothing. She kept walking.
Beside her, Aaliyah bounded across the dunes as if she felt no exhaustion at all.
“Hey, if we stumble across desert baths somewhere around here, I’m signing up for a sand wrap and cactus therapy!” she called enthusiastically, just as they discovered the skeleton of a giant scorpion. “But I’m definitely skipping the scorpion massage.”
Rowena simply laughed.
When night finally came and the moon illuminated the dunes like silver waves, they made camp between two rocky outcrops. Aaliyah lit a fire with the flick of her finger, and Rowena brewed tea from desert herbs she had gathered earlier.
They sat side by side. Silence surrounded them, broken only by the occasional howl of the wind.
“You know most people go mad here within three days?” Aaliyah asked, staring into the flames.
“Because they hear voices,” Rowena replied quietly. “Memories the desert awakens. It has a memory of its own.”
Aaliyah smiled faintly. “Do you hear anything?”
Rowena hesitated. Then she nodded.
“I hear myself. All my doubts. All the moments when I could have saved someone and didn’t make it in time.”
“Those aren’t the desert’s voices,” Aaliyah said, turning to look at her. “That’s your conscience. And it’s so big you could probably suffocate an entire continent with it.”
Rowena sighed softly. “And you? Do you hear anything?”
Aaliyah fell silent.
But the quiet did not last long.
Something began to rise from the sand beneath their feet — large, heavy, scaled. Rowena reacted instantly, spreading a protective barrier around them. From the ground emerged a Sand Lizard, a creature with a maw full of needle-like teeth and eyes like two black pearls.
Aaliyah leapt up, her staff blazing in her hand.
“Stupid lizard,” she hissed.
She was fast. Flames danced around her body as she hurled fire into the beast’s jaws, while Rowena healed every scratch and raised magical barriers to protect their camp.
The lizard fell.
Fried.
Smoke drifted into the sky.
Both of them breathed heavily. The flames slowly died down.
“I’m starting to think you’re cursed, Rowena,” Aaliyah said with a laugh. “Ever since I met you, I haven’t had a single peaceful day.”
“Maybe I am,” Rowena replied. “But you’re the crazy one for staying.”
Aaliyah grinned. “Or maybe I’ve just gotten used to chaos.”
When they sat down again beside the dying fire, both fell silent. In the distance, beyond the dunes, a moving shadow appeared. And with it came a strange, heavy rhythm — footsteps.
“That’s not an animal,” Aaliyah said quietly.
Rowena nodded.
“That’s something else.”
Without a word, they rose to their feet and silently watched the shadow slowly drawing closer.
