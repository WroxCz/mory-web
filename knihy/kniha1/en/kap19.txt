Chapter XIX. - The road to Stonegard Castle
The sand beneath their feet was still cool from the night chill, but the sun was already climbing the sky, and its sharp rays began to burn against their skin. The air warmed quickly, growing heavier, carrying the dusty scent of the desert.
The trio walked southward, toward the distant outline of Stonegard Castle, which rose on the horizon like a dark shadow against the bright blue sky.
Rowena walked in front, the orb in her satchel pulsing gently with silver light, as if breathing in rhythm with her heart. Every step she took was careful, yet steady — as though an invisible thread of fate guided her straight toward the place where she would finally face the truth she sought.
James walked behind them, his eyes sharp and tense, constantly scanning the surroundings, ready to react to any sudden movement or danger.
Aaliyah walked between them, silent and focused, her mind tangled with questions she had not yet found the courage to speak aloud — questions that tightened around her heart.
“Do you feel it?” Rowena asked quietly, her voice barely audible. “The air is changing. Something is close. Something watching us.”
James nodded, his eyes narrowing.
“It’s like darkness waiting around the corner. It isn’t here yet — but it’s waiting for the right moment to strike.”
Then a muffled rustle broke the silence.
The trio froze instantly, their eyes sweeping across the landscape.
Dark spirals began to rise slowly from the sand, creeping toward them with the wind, as if the earth itself had turned against them.
Rowena quickly lifted the orb.
It burst into brilliant white light, its radiance dissolving parts of the dark spirals like the first rays of morning sun melting frost.
“Morr'kalil is already searching for us,” Aaliyah whispered, breathing heavily.
James drew his spear, its tip gleaming in the morning light. “We must keep moving. We cannot hesitate.”
Suddenly, a figure stepped out of the swirling darkness.
Tall. Dark. Surrounded by shadow.
Golden eyes burned like hot coals. Black armor covered with spikes. A sword and spear held firmly in his hands.
Zarveth.
The dark knight whose arrival meant only one thing — destruction and chaos.
Without hesitation, the trio formed a defensive line.
The air around them thickened with heat and tension. The sand beneath their feet seemed alive, drinking in the magic flowing from their bodies.
James gripped his spear tightly, his movements focused and precise. With each thrust, he tried to break through the darkness surrounding Zarveth, striking sharp and accurate blows.
But he soon realized the truth.
The heavy, slow weapon would not help him against an opponent so powerful — and terrifyingly fast.
One of his strikes was blocked effortlessly. With a violent motion, Zarveth knocked the spear aside, forcing James to retreat quickly to keep his balance.
In a flash, James threw the spear aside and drew two concealed daggers, their blades coated with venom.
With daggers in hand, he moved faster — weaving between attacks, striking deadly blows at the weak points in his enemy’s armor.
Aaliyah tightened her grip on her long magical staff, its surface covered with glowing runes pulsing with orange-red light.
From every motion she made, flames erupted — swirling around her like living serpents, twisting and coiling in the air with wild energy.
These fiery creatures devoured the darkness without mercy.
Rowena stood a step behind them, holding an open spellbook and a short wand.
Her magic was quiet, calm — and immensely powerful.
From her touch radiated pearlescent light, forming a protective veil around her companions, healing their wounds, soothing their pain, and strengthening both mind and body so they could stand against the evil before them.
But the battle was not going well.
James, Aaliyah, and Rowena were exhausted.
Their attacks tore at Zarveth’s defenses, yet none were strong enough to deliver a killing blow.
His dark magic swallowed the flames and neutralized the poison on the daggers.
And his laughter grew stronger — echoing like the sound of ruin itself.
The trio began to falter. Their breathing became ragged. Their magic weakened. Their movements slowed. They were close to collapse.
Then, suddenly, Rowena felt warmth at her side.
The orb inside her satchel glowed and pulsed with heat, reacting to Zarveth’s presence.
Without hesitation, she seized it and raised it toward him.
The light intensified.
It spread outward, and with a low hum, a translucent shield formed around the three of them.
Zarveth stopped. His golden eyes fixed not on the shield — but on Rowena.
“So… you are the one who carries it,” he said coldly. His voice carried both contempt and unease.
“And you have even learned how to awaken it.”
With a sudden burst of fury, he lunged forward, focusing all his strength against the barrier, as if he meant to crush not only the shield — but the orb itself.
The blow struck with full force.
The shield trembled violently, its light flickering dangerously.
Rowena gasped.
The orb in her hands grew heavy, its glow pulsing erratically. The magic inside struggled — as if trying to escape.
Thin, dark-silver cracks spread across the surface of the barrier.
With a dull, thunderous sound — the shield shattered.
The orb in Rowena’s hands flashed weakly one last time.
Then it went dark.
Its surface turned cold.
The force of the impact knocked them to their knees.
James fell heavily to the ground, bracing himself with one hand, his daggers trembling in his fingers.
Aaliyah collapsed beside him, her staff resting uselessly in the sand, its flames long extinguished.
Rowena knelt behind them, staring blankly at the lifeless orb, her breath ragged, her body drained of strength.
Zarveth straightened.
The darkness around him grew still, as if sensing the approaching end.
He raised his weapon slowly — deliberately — preparing the final strike.
And in that very moment, when everything seemed lost — a distant thunder rolled across the desert.
Aaliyah gasped.
“Do you hear that?” she whispered.
They froze.
The sound grew louder.
Closer.
Over the dunes appeared silhouettes — dark riders, their armor flashing in the morning light like stars striking metal. Their horses tore through the sand like waves through water, and the clatter of their weapons rang across the desert like the sound of hope.
“That’s…” Aaliyah breathed.
Then she shouted at the top of her lungs, her voice desperate and fierce:
“Help! We’re here! Help!”
The commander of the knights — a man in steel armor with a cloak the color of the night sky — raised his sword and gave the order to charge.
Behind him surged a wave of force.
His riders thundered forward like a flood of light.
Zarveth reacted instantly.
His eyes darkened and turned toward the approaching cavalry and hissed.
That single moment of distraction was enough.
James moved. Silently. Swiftly. The daggers flew.
One struck Zarveth in the face. The second followed a heartbeat later. Steel met inhuman flesh.
Zarveth roared.
His head snapped sideways as dark blood burst from the wound.
He staggered.
The darkness within him faltered — as if the force driving him had briefly lost direction. A surge of black magic erupted from his mouth, only to tear apart into shreds of shadow under the approaching light.
His golden eyes narrowed into slits of burning hatred.
“This is not the end…” he rasped, his voice deep and venomous. “I will return. And when I do… there will be no one left to stop me.”
With a final hiss, he withdrew into the darkness — wounded and humiliated.
His body dissolved into a stream of black mist that sank into the sand, as though the desert itself swallowed him whole.
The air grew heavy after his departure.
Not with peace — but with the silence before a far greater storm.
James, Aaliyah, and Rowena remained standing.
Sweating. Breathless. Wounded. But alive.
The knights of the Allied Resistance of Laslan arrived seconds later.
They reined in their horses sharply, scanning the area, ready to face the enemy.
But Zarveth was already gone.
James dropped to one knee, daggers still in his hands, his breath coming in ragged bursts.
“He didn’t disappear,” he said hoarsely. “He retreated. He knew he couldn’t win — not yet.”
Aaliyah remained silent.
Her staff cooled slowly, its flames reduced to faint embers glowing between the runes.
Rowena, still holding her book, nodded, her eyes fixed on the place where Zarveth had vanished.
“I felt it,” she whispered. “There was something inside him… trying to break free. He is not whole yet. But when he becomes—”
She did not finish.
None of them needed her to.
In the silence that followed the battle, something tense lingered — a promise of darkness that would rise again.
And next time, it would come with far greater power.
