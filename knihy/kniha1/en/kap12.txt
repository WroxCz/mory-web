Chapter XII. - Shadow over the sand
The sun slowly dipped toward the horizon, and the desert bathed in a red-orange glow. Every step in the sand felt heavier, every breath of the dry air sharper. And yet, when the outlines of the oasis finally appeared on the distant horizon, their hearts filled with relief.
The Sanctuary Oasis appeared like a miracle in the middle of the wasteland — nestled between steep cliffs whose ridges rose toward the sky like stone guardians. From a bird’s-eye view, it would resemble an emerald jewel set into the yellow crown of the desert. The smooth rock walls shielded the oasis from fierce winds and prying eyes. And because of this position, it had become a safe harbor for travelers from distant lands.
Among palm groves, a lake of crystal-clear water, and scattered tents stretched a marketplace — bustling and alive like a beating heart. Caravans loaded with goods regularly arrived from surrounding routes. Heavy sacks of spices, fabrics, and precious metals were unloaded from camels, while merchants set up stalls displaying wares from both East and West.
The scent of dried dates, roasted nuts, and spiced tea mingled with the smell of sweat and leather. Every corner of the oasis lived — people haggled, children ran laughing between the stalls, and old storytellers shared tales that carried all the way to the stone sanctuary at its far end.
The Sanctuary Oasis was more than just a place of rest.
It was a crossroads of worlds, a hidden knot of ancient roads, and living proof that even in the middle of nothing, something sacred could be born.
“That’s it… the Sanctuary Oasis,” Aaliyah whispered, her voice a mixture of excitement and exhaustion.
James nodded silently, his eyes still scanning the horizon. Though the beauty of the place was undeniable, his instincts warned him.
Something was wrong.
Rowena stopped at the crest of a dune. She turned into the wind and closed her eyes.
“The air… is different. It smells of magic, but not only the pure kind.”
James stepped closer to her. “I feel it too. It’s like residual energy — twisted and decaying.”
“It’s the Shadow,” Aaliyah said quietly. “It’s been creeping behind us ever since we left the temple.”
Suddenly, the wind rose.
Sand began to swirl upward, forming a column — a vortex of dark dust and shadows. In its center, a vague outline of a face began to take shape — only a shadow, only a flicker — and then two golden eyes ignited within it, piercing and cold.
“Guardians of the orb,” hissed a voice that sounded as if it were made of a thousand whispering voices at once. “Just as foretold. Four seals… four keys… And you — Children of the Light — will bring them directly to the threshold of ruin.”
Aaliyah stepped back. “What are you?”
“I am a messenger. Born from the remnants of Morr'kalil’s will, hidden in the wind, the sand, and the silence between words.”
James stepped in front of the women and gripped the hilt of his dagger.
“Then speak,” he said quietly through clenched teeth. “What exactly did you come here to tell us?”
“A message,” the voice whispered.
Now it sounded deeper. “That you will die soon. Not quickly. Not mercifully…”
James planted his feet more firmly, his eyes dark and unflinching.
“We’ll see about that,” he said quietly.
The Shadow paused for a moment…
Then a cruel laugh erupted from its shapeless form. It sounded hollow, breaking like metal under pressure.
“Yes… we will see,” it hissed, dissolving slowly with a rustling sound like grinding stone.
“The gates are opening… and the Seal is weakening. It is time for the world to taste death once more.”
Silence followed.
“That wasn’t a warning,” Rowena said, frightened. “That was a challenge.”
Rowena tightened her grip on the orb. Its gentle light pulsed like a heartbeat. “We need to reach the Sanctuary Oasis. Maybe we’ll find answers there — where to search for the other orbs… or someone who can show us what to do with them.”
“Or at least buy us time,” James added.
They did not linger.
The path between the dunes began to shorten quickly.
As night approached, the sky turned violet, and the stars ignited like witnesses to their fate.
With every step, the Sanctuary Oasis drew closer — and with it, the next chapter of their journey.
