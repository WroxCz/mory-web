Chapter XX. - Ash on the skin
The sun climbed higher, and its light fell upon the battlefield where the dust had not yet settled. The air was thick with the scent of burning, magic, and iron.
The knights of the Allied Resistance of Laslan dismounted from their horses, their heavy steps leaving deep marks in the sand. Some rushed immediately toward the trio standing at the center of the scorched circle, while others cautiously searched the surroundings, prepared for another attack.
The commander of the knights — a tall, scarred man with blond hair, a full beard, and blue eyes — approached them with both respect and unease.
His armor was covered in dust, his swords once again sheathed across his back. “We received the message. Harim’s hawk reached our camp at dawn. We rode out immediately.”
Aaliyah nodded, her eyes fixed on the horizon where the sand still trembled faintly. “Fortunately, you arrived just in time. A moment longer, and Zarveth would have killed us.”
Rowena knelt nearby.
The orb in her palm glowed with a dim light, as if it too were recovering from exhaustion. Sweat beaded across her forehead, mixed with dust and ash clinging to her skin like a reminder of the clash.
“He lost strength, but not resolve,” she murmured without looking up. “He will return. And next time, he will not come alone.”
James sat beside her, his daggers already put away, his spear left far behind them. His eyes were sharp, but his spirit carried a weight — the knowledge that even three against one had not been enough.
“He was faster than anything I’ve ever faced,” he said quietly. “And that wasn’t even his full potential.”
The knights began distributing supplies — water, bandages, healing elixirs. A young squire offered Rowena his hand, but she shook her head softly and rose on her own.
Her movements were unsteady, yet her eyes remained firm. “We must reach Stonegard,” she said quietly, almost to herself. “Before something worse than Zarveth emerges from the darkness.”
“If the seal is breaking, then this was only the first strike,” Aaliyah agreed, quietly wiping blood from her fingers.
Her magical staff lay beside her, the runes on its surface still faintly glowing, as if the magic remembered the heat that had surged through it.
James stood and helped Aaliyah to her feet.
“We travel in silence,” he said. “The darkness that followed us may hear more than we think.”
When the trio set out again — now under the protection of the knights and mounted on horses provided for them — their progress became faster.
And above them, high among the clouds, a solitary hawk flew back toward the Sanctuary Oasis, carrying news of what had happened.
