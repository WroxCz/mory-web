Chapter IV. - The girl who burned
Flashback from James’s perspective
James stood leaning against a tent pole, cleaning the blade of his dagger. He ran a cloth along the edge slowly and carefully. All the while, he discreetly watched the woman sitting by the fire, laughing at something William had said.
Aaliyah.
She was like fire. Honest, wild, and kind. There was always hope in her voice.
And beside her? William.
His fellow warrior. Confident, eloquent, well-liked. Whenever he entered a room, he drew attention like a magnet — and Aaliyah was no exception. In battle, reliable. In camp, loud. In love… selfish.
James saw it clearly. William did not love her — he enjoyed being loved.
Aaliyah began to fade. Not in body — but in spirit. There was more pain than light in her magic. Her laughter slowly turned into a strained mask of cheerfulness. And James, always just a few steps behind her, silently watched as her fire began to smolder.
One morning, he saw her standing before an empty tent.
William had vanished.
No farewell. No apology. Only silence and cold.
Aaliyah did not move. She did not need words. She needed someone who could stand beside her in silence.
So that night, he came to her fire.
“You deserve more than to be someone’s trophy,” he told her quietly.
She lowered her head. The silence between them was fragile.
“What do you want, James?” she asked sharply. “Reassurance that you’re better than him?”
He shook his head.
“I want… to protect you. Not like a savior. Just like someone who sees you for who you are. Not because of what you do for others.”
It was the bravest thing he had ever said. And at the same time, the truth he had carried within himself for a long time.
Her eyes filled with something he recognized immediately — fear, pain, and a quiet no.
“Don’t say that, James… Please,” she whispered.
He respected her decision. He did not try to persuade her. Instead, he looked into her eyes one last time.
“I want you to know I expect nothing. But… for a long time I’ve watched you burn for others. And if someone were to be the one who protects you — I would want it to be me. I’m not pressuring you,” he said in a quiet voice. “You don’t have to answer me. You don’t have to decide today, or tomorrow. Just think about it.”
Before the gates of Vienta, he looked into her eyes one last time and walked away.
With her face in his memory. With the knowledge that he had not been chosen. And yet with a quiet faith that what existed between them — or what could have existed — was not yet lost.
When, two years later, he saw a flame in the desert, he knew immediately it was her.
Her fire could not be mistaken for any other. Not even after all that time.
She stood with her magical staff in her hand, ready to strike, her gaze fixed directly on him.
