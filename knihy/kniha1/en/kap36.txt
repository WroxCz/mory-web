Chapter XXXVI – The Past

Herba was a city of shining armor and heroic deeds. A city where the future of the realm was decided, and where every knight longed for recognition. And among them was one who shone brighter than the rest — William.

He was young, handsome, incredibly talented with sword and shield, and gifted as a tactician. People loved him — men admired him, women sought him out. Wherever he appeared, whispers and laughter followed in his wake. In taverns, fencing halls, and temple courtyards, stories about him circulated, often blending admiration with envy.

And William? He savored every moment.

By day, he trained with his unit. By night, he enjoyed the company of different women. He never promised, never committed. And in the end, that was what betrayed him.

One evening, he ended up in bed with a woman who should have been beyond his reach — Elarin, the sister of one of the senior commanders of the knightly guard. For him, it was just another passionate night without obligations. But for her… it meant something more. When, a few days later, he began to ignore her and eventually rejected her coldly, Elarin changed.

Vindictive and wounded, she turned to her brother — and he acted without mercy.

William was “reassigned” to Vienta, a quiet, insignificant town on the very edge of the province. Officially, it was only a temporary measure, but everyone knew it was punishment. A stripping away of glory, position, and access to power.

At first, he resisted. He sent messages, requested hearings. But nothing changed. Vienta was calm, almost sleepy. No wars, no heroic expeditions. Only patrols, reports, and long, silent nights.

And in that silence, he met Aaliyah.

She was beautiful and strong. Their relationship was like lightning — fast, intense, dangerous. They spent passionate nights together, yet even that was not enough. William was restless, torn inside. He did not want to live in obscurity; he wanted more. Glory, power, recognition — everything that had been taken from him. And night after night, bitterness grew within him.

Until one night…

As he sat alone in his chamber, staring into emptiness, something moved in the shadow by the wall — something that should not have been there.

A figure without a face, black as an abyss, appeared silently, without warning. Its voice was a whisper, yet it spoke directly into his mind.

“You are dissatisfied. Forgotten. Insulted. But I see you.”

William did not speak. He only froze, gripped by emotions he could not name.

“Do you want back what belongs to you?” the shadow continued.
“Do you want more than they could ever offer you?”

“And what do you want in return?” William asked quietly.

“You will help me. When the time comes, you will weaken the seals. You will give darkness the key. Nothing more.”

And William — a disappointed, broken man with anger and betrayal smoldering in his heart — nodded.

He did not know exactly what would come, but one thing he knew for certain:

He would do anything to rise again.
To make the world whisper his name once more.

In the days that followed, he behaved as if nothing had happened. He fulfilled his duties in peaceful Vienta, continued seeing Aaliyah, and maintained a calm mask in front of others. Only at night, when he was alone, did he feel something stirring within him. The shadows in the corner of the room moved differently than they should. Sometimes, he heard whispers in his mind.

Then the message came.

It was evening when a scroll bearing the command seal was placed into his hands. The message was short but powerful.

“The knights of Herba have taken note of your discipline and results. Your request has been reconsidered. You are promoted. If you accept, send a hawk. You will be welcomed at Stonegard Castle.”

A slow smile spread across his face.

The glory once taken from him was returning. And deep down, he knew it was no coincidence. That what he had accepted had already begun to shape the course of the world itself.

He did not hesitate. That very night, he sent his reply.

At the time, they were camped at an oasis due to a minor conflict along the border with nomadic clans. They were supposed to remain there for several more days. But William knew he no longer belonged to that forgotten place.

That night, he returned once more to Aaliyah’s tent.

They made love passionately, as if it were to be the last time. And for him, it truly was.

At dawn, while she still slept, he dressed and left the tent without a sound. Before departing completely, he returned to her side and left a folded piece of paper on her pillow.

“It’s over.”

The journey to Stonegard took several days.

William arrived tired, but ready. Here, in the high halls and among elite warriors, he finally felt he was where he belonged.

And it was here that he met Thomas.

Strong. Honorable. Direct. Thomas was everything William had long since ceased to be. But perhaps that was exactly why they became friends.

Through training, missions, and long nights on watch, a strong bond formed between them.

Thus began his new chapter.

A knight of Stonegard — virtuous on the surface… but carrying a dark secret deep beneath it.
