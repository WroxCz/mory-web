Chapter XVI. - Silver in the depths
Night descended upon the Sanctuary Oasis like a heavy silk veil.
The moon cast a pale, mysterious light across the surface of the lake, reflecting in uneven ripples. The surrounding tents swayed gently in the night breeze, their canvas whispering softly in rhythm with the wind, as if they themselves were breathing.
From afar came muted sounds — the faint chime of small metal bells, distant laughter, and the hushed voices of merchants. The scent of spices and mint tea drifted upward into the sky.
And yet, a tension lingered in the air — invisible, heavy.
Something dark, hostile was slowly approaching, and though it was not yet visible, everyone could feel it deep in their bones.
James sat on a rocky ledge not far from the tent, his legs folded beneath him, elbows resting on his knees. His gaze was fixed on the distance, where darkness seeped into the sand and clutched the golden dunes like grasping fingers.
In his mind, the images from the Spring of Knowledge repeated again and again.
A chill crept through his body.
It was not only fear, but the awareness of a burden — a destiny older than his own life, ancient as the desert itself.
Behind him came the faint rustle of movement. He did not need to turn to know who approached.
Aaliyah.
She sat quietly beside him without a word. In her hands, she held two small, carefully wrapped objects. She unfolded one and placed it between them.
It was a red stone — smooth and polished like a drop of blood — set into a silver frame.
A talisman from her homeland. “For protection,” she whispered, her voice barely audible.
“A red stone from the Raging Wilderness. They say it can recognize truth hidden behind lies.”
James watched it silently at first, observing how it pulsed in the moonlight, as if it possessed a life of its own. “Thank you,” he said at last. “I think this place… that spring… showed me more than I wanted to see. We need more than protection.”
Aaliyah nodded, her gaze drifting toward the lake, whose waters now seemed like a living organism. “Lysara warned us,” she whispered. “Morr'kalil — that ancient darkness — is awakening. The seal we felt in the desert is losing its strength.”
“And we must stop that darkness,” James added.
Aaliyah looked at him for a long moment.
Silence between them grew heavy.
She drew a deep breath and scanned the surroundings.

Rowena stood at the edge of the lake, her figure blending into the dark shadows of the fig trees whose branches bent toward the water like ancient guardians.
Her eyes, reflected in the surface, did not see only her own image — but something more.
She felt the water shifting gently beneath her fingers, as if responding to her presence. It wrapped softly around her hand, caressing it, drawing her closer with a kindness that felt almost maternal.
“Rowena?” a voice called behind her.
Harim stood a short distance away, his eyes calm but filled with gravity.
She did not turn.
“The water… speaks to me,” she said slowly, with unexpected certainty. “It told me where I must go.”
Harim watched the surface for a moment. “The Spring of Knowledge is older than we can imagine. It remembers things that time itself has long forgotten. If it truly speaks to you, listen — and let it guide you.”
Rowena tightened her grip on her orb. Its light had dimmed now, like the breath of a sleeping creature.
“We leave tomorrow,” she said calmly.
Harim nodded. “Good. The darkness sleeping beneath our feet is awakening. It is searching for its children.”
That night, none of them truly slept.
Aaliyah stroked her talisman, searching for certainty within its stone.
James lay on his back with a dagger in his hand, his mind wandering between the past and an unknown future.
Rowena sat in the dim light of the tent, eyes open, the orb resting on her lap, pulsing faintly.
Outside, the wind strengthened.
And the lake — silver and restless — made a quiet, ominous lapping sound.

Kayan, a young guard standing at the northern gate of the oasis, felt unease growing inside him.
Despite the cool night air, sweat slowly trickled down his back.
He had the disturbing sensation that something was approaching.
Instinctively, he called into the darkness:
“Who’s there?”
No one answered.
Only silence. And then — a whisper.
A foreign language he could not understand with his mind, yet somehow grasped every word. As if he had known it since birth.
“Open.”
His heart began to pound. He took a step backward.
“Let me enter.”
Cold wrapped around his throat like an invisible hand. He could not breathe. And then the darkness came. The Shadow consumed him.
Kayan’s body tensed, his eyes widened — and their color changed.
Liquid gold filled his irises until his gaze ceased to be human.
A breath escaped his lips — deep, as it no longer belonged to him.
The shadows around him thickened and transformed into a twisted black armor covered with spikes, a spear, and a sword. The weapons were heavy and unbalanced. They were not meant for humans. Every movement suggested they could split a man in half with a single strike. And yet Kayan wielded them with terrifying ease.
Kayan was no longer Kayan.
He had become Zarveth.
His gaze was empty, his eyes golden and without emotion. From beneath his armor radiated a cold power that did not belong to this world.
Zarveth straightened and swept his gaze across the dark landscape of the oasis.
And he waited quietly…for his time to come.
