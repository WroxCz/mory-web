Chapter V. - Fire, shadow, and water
The desert wind changed.
Rowena felt it first — not physically, but within her magic. The waves of time and pain that had once breathed from the sand grew quiet. In their place came pressure. Invisible. Focused. A force that belonged neither to the living nor the dead.
Something in between.
The figure slowly approached.
A long spear in his hand, daggers at his belt, and light black armor. Short dark hair tousled by the wind. A face like stone, covered with short stubble, and black eyes that carried death within them.
Beside her, Aaliyah stopped moving. Her fingers tightened gently around her staff — but not for defense.
For him.
“Who is that?” Rowena whispered.
Aaliyah nodded. “James.”
When he finally reached them, he stood in silence for a moment. He knew very well what a single wrong word could cause.
Aaliyah moved first. Slowly, tension in her shoulders, but no fear in her eyes. Only old uncertainty.
“You took your time,” she said.
“I followed the tracks,” he replied quietly.
“Mine?”
“Yours. And the tracks of something that shouldn’t be here.”
Rowena felt the tension between them — strong, old, unnamed. She wanted to step back, but remained. Not as an intruder, but as a witness. As someone who needed to hear all of this.
James quietly sat down by the fire opposite Aaliyah. His black armor rustled softly as he placed his hands on his knees and stared into the flames without a word. His face was half hidden in shadow, but his eyes — dark and deep — followed the dance of the fire with a strange calm.
Aaliyah lowered her gaze and began to toy with the strap on her boot.
“What really brought you here, James?” Rowena finally asked, unable to hold back her curiosity.
“Darkness,” he answered. “Someone — or something — has crawled out from the depths beneath the Moonlight Desert. I can feel it. And the fact that you two are here is no coincidence.”
“So now you hunt shadows instead of people?” Aaliyah smirked.
“Shadows don’t have souls. They don’t scream when they die,” he replied flatly.
Aaliyah lowered her eyes. She had no answer to that.
That very night, the desert tore open.
Not with sound — but with presence.
From beneath the ground emerged the first night creature — a being formed of sand, bones, and magic older than the desert itself. Its eyes burned blue. It made a sound that ripped at the ears.
All three of them rose.
Aaliyah raised her staff. Rowena whispered a protective spell behind her. And James, without a single word, lifted his spear and charged forward —
a shadow between flame and light.
