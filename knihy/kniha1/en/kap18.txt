Chapter XVIII. - The road into shadow
Morning came quietly.
A cold wind rose over the desert, carrying fine grains of sand that whispered against the fabric of the tents. The oasis still slept, but at its heart an uneasy spark was already smoldering, ready to ignite.
James stood at the entrance of the tent, watching the horizon where the first rays of sunlight slowly began to appear. Beside him, Rowena prepared for the journey.
Aaliyah woke last.
Her body was still tired, but her mind was working at full speed. She knew that everything that had happened was no coincidence. The oasis, the Spring of Knowledge, and Zarveth, slowly creeping through the shadows — all of it had woven itself into a single, inevitable thread of fate.
Harim was waiting for them at the southern gate.
He leaned on his staff, his face serious, yet his eyes held the calm of someone accustomed to farewells.
“Take care of one another. Darkness walks behind you,” he said quietly.
Rowena stepped forward and gave a short bow. “Thank you for the shelter… and for the warning.”
Aaliyah smiled — this time without her usual sharpness. “Keep the spring safe, Harim. I would hate to return to ashes.”
Harim allowed himself a faint smile. “The spring will be here, waiting. I will protect it with my own life.”
“We must hurry,” James spoke in a low voice. “Darkness does not wait, and the road to Stonegard is long.”
With those words, they stepped into the morning light.
Each step carried the weight of destiny — but also the determination not to break.
Behind them remained the oasis — the place where their battle had begun, and where shadows still lingered, waiting to consume them.
