Chapter VII. - A Fragment of Time
As soon as the sun rose high above the horizon, its rays ceased to warm — they burned. Heat poured down from the sky without mercy, and the ground beneath them trembled, as if every touch of the blazing air were a blow. The air shimmered in wavering currents, heavy with tension. Nature fell into an unnatural silence, as though the whole world were holding its breath.
And yet… a quiet, deep sound emerged.
It sounded like a breath taken far beneath their feet.
Then the earth groaned.
Not with the cry of magic, as one might expect. There was no glow of runes, no scent of burned herbs. Nothing that could be written into a book. The sound was raw — human, and yet not human at all.
As if the earth itself were suffering.
Not physically, but… from memory.
A memory of war.
Not a war fought for land or crown — but a war fought for ritual. A dark ceremony filled with pain and despair, where countless human sacrifices were not a byproduct, but the very purpose.
The earth remembered.
Screams. Blood seeping into the soil.
And now, after years of silence, that memory began to awaken.
“Do you feel it?” Aaliyah asked quietly, as if afraid her voice might break something fragile.
She stood a step ahead of the others, her eyes narrowed against the sun, her brow lightly damp — not from heat, but from something deeper, internal. She felt her stomach twist, her heart beating uneasily, like a bell warning of an approaching storm.
Rowena turned slowly and carefully. Her hair moved in a wind that should not have been there.
“That isn’t magic,” she said in a dark voice. “It’s a Fragment of Time.”
“A Fragment of Time?” Aaliyah asked, confused.
“Yes,” Rowena continued. “They are fragments of memories that the earth carries within itself like scars. Sometimes those memories awaken — they become tangible, alive. As if the land itself wanted to remind us of what once was… and warn us of what may come.”
“But who could—” Aaliyah began.
Suddenly, the ground split apart with a terrifying crack — and darkness swallowed them.
In the depths of blackness, everything began to change.
The shadows thickened and started to ripple like dense fog, slowly forming into images of long-forgotten events.
The picture of the past blurred before them, colors fading into dullness.
Time broke.
The landscape around them shifted, and they found themselves standing in the darkest period of history — in the war against a fanatical cult, nearly five centuries ago.
It was an age when the land cried beneath the weight of its people’s suffering. Every drop of blood, every scream of pain seeped into its core like poison. The soil twisted in agony, cracking and trembling, as if it felt every malicious act unfolding above it. Nature itself begged for mercy.
The world had nearly fallen to its knees before a god whose name was forbidden to be spoken.
Morr’kalil.
The dark god of blood, suffering, and death.
He knew neither compassion nor mercy. He found deep satisfaction in human suffering. He longed for a world that would be nothing but an endless wound — one that would never heal.
His followers believed the world was irredeemably corrupted, and that only through suffering could it be purified. Morr’kalil was their truth, their law, their savior in flame.
He dwelled in the Darkness — in an empty place beyond the boundary of death, where lost souls drowned in the fog of oblivion.
He fed on them.
Their pain sustained him.
But he wanted more.
He wanted the living.
He wanted to turn the entire world into a burning temple of agony.
His followers spread across the world like a plague. They sought four sacred places where the boundary between this world and Morr’kalil’s realm was thinnest, bringing sacrifices there.
It did not matter who was sacrificed.
Morr’kalil did not choose.
Everyone was suitable.
Anyone who had a tongue to cry for help and a body that could bleed.
The victims were dragged to altars — chains on their wrists, heads forced to the ground. They screamed, begged for mercy, gasped for breath as terror twisted their faces.
And around them… the crowd danced.
Fanatics stood in a circle, their faces distorted by savage madness and religious zeal that consumed every part of their being. Around their necks hung heavy amulets — cold, dark metal discs engraved with a lidless eye that watched every movement with merciless emptiness.
Their chanting was ecstatic, almost joyful — transformed into a prayer of pain and destruction, born from their belief that every cut and every drop of blood was a sacred act cleansing the world.
They laughed cruelly, with a fury that tore the soul and twisted the heart in the darkness of their fanatic devotion.
Their pupils were wide and animal-like, burning with unyielding faith in Morr’kalil as the only savior and judge.
The bodies of the victims were mutilated with such precision that it seemed almost… beautiful.
But it was not beauty for the world of humans.
It was beauty for Morr’kalil.
The shadows moved.
The ground ran red.
The air burned.
And the silence between screams carried the weight of stone.
Nothing ever grew in those places again. Magic fractured. Light avoided the soil. The air tasted like ash. Time itself seemed to vanish.
And in the darkest hour — when three rituals had already been completed and the fourth was meant to crown Morr’kalil’s return — a desperate act was born.
The Resistance.
Three continents — Laslan, Stonegard, and Talandre — united their forces for the first time in history, though each saw the darkness differently. They were not bound by hope, but by desperation.
Desperation born from the knowledge that if they failed, the world would drown in endless suffering.
It was a war unlike any before.
Not for power.
Not for land.
Not for faith.
But for the very soul of the world — for its right to sunlight without pain, for love without destruction, for death without the mockery of the dark god Morr’kalil.
Battles raged across endless plains, through tangled underground tunnels, upon storm-torn seas, and in the skies above. Ancient cities that had witnessed centuries fell in flames.
Tens of thousands of warriors — knights, mages, and mercenaries — died in blood, among shadows, smoke, and screams.
And at last came the final battle in the Swamp of Silence, a place where the currents of time, memory, and will converged — where the boundary was as thin as paper.
There, on that threshold of the world, the fate of all living things was decided.
The army of the Resistance repelled the last followers of Morr’kalil, who came like visions of death.
They performed the sealing ritual — a complex and dangerous ceremony meant to drive the dark god back into the abyss from which he had risen.
The gate between the world and the darkness closed.
Time in the Swamp of Silence shattered and twisted.
Those who survived began to see things that should never have been seen. Memory faltered and rewrote itself. Past and present intertwined in a terrifying dance. The line between reality and madness dissolved.
But Morr’kalil… did not forget.
