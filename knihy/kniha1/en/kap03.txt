Chapter III. - Flame and shadow
Flashback from Aaliyah’s perspective
Golden light from the setting sun fell over the camp, where the euphoria of another victorious expedition slowly dissolved into dust and weary gazes. Aaliyah sat on a rock with her legs crossed, her head resting in her palms. Her robes were scorched from magic, her hands exhausted, and her heart beat faster than it should have.
Behind her, she heard a voice she knew all too well.
“You overdid it again, Red. That’s what happens when you try to save the whole world by yourself.”
Aaliyah smiled, even though she didn’t want to — William. Eternally sarcastic and charming. He could make the entire unit laugh even in the hardest moments. His touch was hot, his words confident. He always had an answer for everything. He was a great friend, but a very selfish lover.
He approached her still in armor, a shield on his back and a sword at his side. His short brown hair was slightly tousled, his neatly trimmed beard framing a distinctive, lightly tanned face with a smile that looked like an invitation — and perhaps a mockery.
Aaliyah turned away and muttered dryly, “Well, if I hadn’t used my magic to the fullest, we’d probably all be dead right now… so thanks for the concern, hero.”
William laughed. “I’m glad you’re so devoted… must have been exhausting, huh?” he said, tilting his head in a teasing way.
Then he sat down beside her, close enough that she could feel his warmth. He leaned toward her ear.
“I hope you’ve still got a little energy left… for tonight.”
Aaliyah raised an eyebrow and frowned, but a faint smile quickly spread across her face.
That was how it went between them for several months — teasing, light flirting, passionate nights, and tension that never truly faded.
And then one day she woke up, and he was gone.
No goodbye. No explanation. On the pillow lay only a brief note:
“I’m done with you.”
Aaliyah picked it up, her hands trembling. The words were short, cold — but the pain they caused was fiery and endless.
Her heart pounded so hard she felt as if she could hear her own pulse. Instinctively, she ran from her bed into his tent, hoping he would be there — that maybe it had all been some stupid joke.
But the tent was empty. All his belongings were gone.
Aaliyah stood before the empty tent for a long time. Tears filled her eyes, and her heart ached with a pain that burned like an unquenchable flame.
In the distance, James watched her. He leaned against a small rock, hidden in the shadows. The warrior said nothing, but his eyes spoke. Aaliyah noticed him and knew he had seen everything — how she laughed, how she loved, and how she fell.
That evening, when she sat alone by the fire, James approached quietly. He sat beside her, and after a long silence, he said:
“You deserve more than to be someone’s trophy.”
Aaliyah studied him. His gaze was different. There was no pity in it. But there was admiration. Respect. And something quiet that pierced her more deeply than William’s betrayal.
“What do you want, James?” she asked. “Reassurance that you’re better than him?”
Anger shot into her voice before she could stop it. She knew she was shouting at the wrong person. It wasn’t his fault. It never had been.
He simply sat there. Calm and sincere.
And that was what irritated her the most.
He shook his head.
“I want… to protect you. Not like a savior. Just like someone who sees you for who you are. Not because of what you do for others.”
His words caught her off guard. For a moment, she just stared in silence. She realized what he was truly offering — genuine feelings.
“Don’t say that, James… Please,” she whispered.
“I want you to know I expect nothing. But… for a long time I’ve watched you burn for others. And if someone were to be the one who protects you — I’d want it to be me,” he replied calmly.
In that moment, her heart tightened, because she knew that if she allowed it… he could betray her too.
James fell silent for a moment, then slowly lowered his gaze.
“I’m not pressuring you,” he added quietly. “You don’t have to answer me. You don’t have to decide today, or tomorrow.”
He lifted his eyes to hers — calm, without demand.
“Just think about it.”
Then he stepped back. Just one step. As if that single movement closed everything that had been said between them. He turned and walked into the night, leaving her by the fire — in the warmth, in the crackling flames, with all the thoughts burning inside her chest.
Two days later, they stood at the gate of Vienta, where the unit parted ways.
“Forgive me, James…” she said.
He nodded. Without anger. Without insistence.
“I’ll come back. When you want me to.”
And he left.
For two years, Aaliyah gathered the pieces of her heart and tried to put them back together. By day, she ate, fought, and smiled — a perfect illusion she showed to the world. But night was her downfall. When everything around her grew silent and her body rested on the bed, her mind filled with painful memories of two men who would not leave her in peace.
The first man was like a magnet, drawing everyone around him. Dominant. Unshakable in his stance. He controlled her with a gaze that burned hotter than the desert sun. His touch had the power to ignite a fire within her so fierce that she forgot everything else.
Those were the moments when it felt as if the whole world belonged to her — when she was his obsession, his private game.
But all of it was only an illusion that vanished with the dawn. His passion was merely a fleeting flame that burned everything in its path, leaving nothing behind but ashes.
Aaliyah often felt like a toy he had discarded once he grew bored. His words were sharp as a blade, often careless and cruel. He was a man who played with emotions as if they were objects to manipulate, and no one — not even her — was more than a tool for his momentary need.
And yet, although he drove her to the edge of despair, he left a mark that still burned within her — pain, and the memory of moments when passion was so strong it stole her breath.
The second man in her life was the very opposite of a storm.
He was quiet as twilight creeping across the land. Full of mystery, silent and deep like a well Aaliyah had long been afraid to look into. He spoke little — sometimes remaining silent for hours, simply watching her with a gaze that held not only passion, but something far more dangerous.
Despite his restraint, every gesture of his cried tenderness — he handed her water before she could ask for it. He gave her a blanket even when he himself must have been cold. He never pushed when she lacked the strength to speak.
And in that restraint, in his gentle touch without demand, in his silence that was not empty but full of meaning, Aaliyah learned for the first time what it meant to be wanted — not for her body, but for her soul.
