Chapter XXXIII – The Double Face

By day, Brienne was the embodiment of innocence and fragility. She walked quietly through the castle corridors, always with a faint smile meant to inspire sympathy and trust. Her voice was soft and shy, her words chosen carefully so as not to disturb anyone. No one doubted that the delicate girl was merely a victim who had survived something horrific.

Everyone pitied her. Knights escorted her with respect, mages offered their help, and even the most hardened guards could not hide the compassion in their eyes when they saw her sitting alone in a corner of the dining hall.

But the night belonged to an entirely different Brienne.

Once the castle fell silent, when the last lights faded and the guards took their posts, Brienne slipped quietly from her chamber. In the shadows of dark corridors and stairways, her figure changed. From a soft, vulnerable girl, she became an invisible hunter. Her fingers turned into claws, made for killing. Her eyes glowed in the darkness with a green light that left nothing to chance.

This time, in the kitchen, she noticed a young man working there — barely grown, full of dreams and hope, yet unaware of the danger lurking in every corner of the castle. His naivety and trust made him the perfect instrument in her hands.

The boy noticed her the moment she leaned lightly against the kitchen doorway. Her figure seemed fragile, like a lost girl trying to bear something too heavy for her strength. In her eyes was feigned pain and helplessness, barely concealed.

“What are you doing here alone by the kitchen?” he asked quietly, his voice gentle and full of concern.

Brienne looked at him with an uncertain smile.
“I got lost,” she admitted softly.
“I was deep in my thoughts… and lost my way inside them.”

The boy stepped closer with a kind smile.

“I’m here if you want to talk,” he said quietly.
“With someone who will truly listen.”

Brienne lifted her eyes to him, where weariness and hope seemed to mingle.

“No one has ever listened to me… But you… you are the first who tries to understand,” she whispered faintly.

A small spark of hope lit her gaze.

She leaned closer, gently touching his arm as if seeking support.

“Come with me somewhere no one will hear us.”

With her look, she invited him to follow, and her smile promised tenderness that was at once dangerously enchanting.

Her closeness intoxicated him. His sense of reality dissolved in her presence. Slowly, she led him through the corridors and down into the underground tunnels, where no one could hear their footsteps.

Brienne guided him to the railing of an old stone stairway, where their kisses grew more passionate. Her fingers moved across his chest, caressing him gently while igniting a flame of desire that bound him to her.

Their bodies moved together in a silent dance — carefully slow, yet filled with fierce intensity. Every kiss, every embrace carried both tenderness and the deeper game Brienne played on the edge of life and death.

As the passion reached its peak, the young man forgot the world outside — all his fears and doubts. In her arms, he found a home he had never known.

But as the passion faded, Brienne tightened her grip around his wrist.

Her eyes darkened, transforming into deep, merciless abysses.

Slowly, ominously, shadowy claws grew from her fingers — gleaming and deadly.

The young man felt sharp pain as the venomous claws cut into his flesh. His body stiffened, his breath caught. Desire turned into terror.

Brienne watched as life slowly left him. His pulse weakened… and then stopped.

Then, on the stone floor around his lifeless body, she drew a symbol with black ash — an eye without a lens surrounded by runes, a mark that opened the path to darkness.

“A sacrifice for Lord Morr'kalil,” she whispered, her voice like ice.
“May his strength grow, and may the seal break.”

Then, with inhuman force, she lunged at the motionless body. Flesh yielded beneath her grip, and the silence of the underground tunnel was torn apart by the dull, wet sound of tearing tissue. Without hesitation, without mercy, she ripped him apart as if it were merely another necessary part of the ritual.

Blood spread across the stone floor and soaked into the drawn runes, which briefly glimmered darkly, as if responding to her act.

Then, without a word, she turned and vanished into the shadows of the tunnels, leaving behind ruined youth and death.

And somewhere far away, in the depths of the desert, Morr'kalil slowly smiled — his power growing, while the world beneath his influence gradually bowed its head before the approaching darkness.
