Chapter XVII. - Where the water leads
The sound of the curtain announced Rowena’s return.
Her face was paler than usual, her movements quiet, as if she had come back from a place where voices were never raised. Aaliyah sat on her heels, wrapped in a light blanket, while James stood with his arms crossed over his chest.
“The water knows me,” Rowena said softly, but with certainty. “It speaks to me.”
Aaliyah shivered. “What did it tell you?”
“That evil is awakening, and the answers do not lie here. It showed me a fragment of a map.”
James frowned. “A fragment of a map? Did you recognize the place? Did it tell you anything else?”
Rowena shook her head. “No. It told me nothing more. We must go to Stonegard Castle. The answers await us there.”

At that very moment, Harim entered the tent.
His face was grave, and in his eyes burned a deep, pulsing fear. Slowly, he looked over Aaliyah, James, and Rowena, as if measuring whether they were ready to hear what he was about to say.
Aaliyah pulled the blanket tighter around her body. James remained motionless, his face tense, eyes fixed on Harim. Rowena stared at him steadily, gripping the orb in her hand.
Harim drew a deep breath.
Slowly and quietly, he began. “You must be prepared. What happened tonight changes everything — not only for us, but for the entire world as we know it.” He paused for a moment, then continued. “The patrol that came to relieve two young guards at the northern gate found one of them dead. He lay there, torn to pieces and covered in blood. His eyes were wide open, as if the terror he felt before dying had been frozen inside them forever.”
Harim rubbed his temples, and for a moment his gaze lost focus in the darkness of memory. “And the other boy…” he continued quietly,“…stood over the body. But he was no longer the young man they knew. The patrol barely recognized him — not because of the blood on him. His appearance had changed so deeply that he seemed foreign, unreal. His skin was black as coal. His eyes were golden — like liquid metal. There was nothing human left in them, only emptiness and an icy calm. He wore armor black as a moonless night, covered with sharp spikes. In his hands he held a sword and a spear, both formed from pure darkness. The sword was broad and heavy, its blade swallowing all light, as if it had devoured hope itself. The spear was long and jagged, like a thorn grown from the deepest abyss — a brutal and merciless instrument of pain.”
Harim lifted his eyes. Calm and terror wrestled within his expression. “He did not speak. He only looked around. Then he slowly turned and stepped into the shadow. He did not leave — he vanished. As if darkness itself had accepted him and hidden him in its endless embrace.”
Silence filled the tent.
Harim’s words hung in the air like an invisible cloud of darkness pressing against every heart.
Aaliyah pulled the blanket tightly around her shoulders and stared into the shadow in the corner of the tent. Her hands trembled slightly, though she tried to appear calm.
“How is that possible?” she whispered at last, her voice fragile. “How can someone be so… changed?”
James stiffened, his hands clenching into fists, his expression growing severe.
“This is no longer just a threat from the outside,” he said. “This is something creeping inward — among us. Something that takes our people and turns them into something… that has no place in the light.”
Aaliyah slowly rose to her feet, her eyes burning with determination.
“If that is no longer the boy we knew, then we must be even more careful. And we must act quickly. The darkness that consumed him may consume others as well.”
Her gaze met Harim’s.
“What now?”
Harim nodded, his expression still grave, yet a trace of resolve entered his voice.
“We must prepare for the worst. Stonegard Castle is our only hope — there you may find answers, and strength. But the road there will be dangerous. And whatever has already escaped may follow your every step.”
The air inside the tent seemed to grow heavier still.
Each of them felt the weight of the inevitable — the knowledge that their world would never be the same again, and that the darkness they had once sensed only as a whisper was now becoming a roaring presence close at hand.
Aaliyah slowly nodded and exhaled. “Then we must be strong. For ourselves — and for all who still believe that light can prevail.”
James straightened and cast a firm glance toward the entrance of the tent, where night swallowed everything beyond. “We leave at dawn.”
Aaliyah sat back down, her gaze fixed on the darkness outside. “Whatever comes,” she said quietly,
“we will not be alone. Light will find its way — even in the shadows.”
