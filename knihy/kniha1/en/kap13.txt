Chapter XIII. - Sand and silver
Dawn was breaking when they finally reached the Sanctuary Oasis.
Aaliyah wiped dust from her forehead and shaded her eyes with a sweaty palm. From a distance, the place had looked like a mirage — like a dream woven from palm trees, shimmering water, and stone towers. But as they descended the winding path down from the cliffs, the dream slowly turned into reality.
The air was still dry, yet noticeably cooler than in the open wasteland. The surrounding rocks provided protection from the relentless heat. A gentle wind flowed along the stone road, carrying with it the scent of date palms, smoke, and spices. The murmur of voices, the clinking of metal, and the calls of caravans echoed through the air.
James walked ahead, his gaze focused, holding his weapon firmly in one hand while offering the other to Rowena, who stumbled slightly on the gravel slope. Aaliyah walked at his other side, watching the life pulsing through the oasis.
When they reached the entrance, two local guards in colorful scarves studied them from a distance but offered no resistance. Strangers were a common sight in the oasis, as long as they did not disturb its balance. And the three of them looked more exhausted than dangerous — though their eyes revealed they carried more than just the dust of travel.
“Water…” Rowena whispered when she spotted the lake surrounded by the shade of a fig tree. Her voice sounded almost reverent.
“And maybe a bed,” Aaliyah added tiredly, but with a faint smile.
They sat down on the soft sand at the edge of the marketplace. Around them flowed a stream of people — men in turbans, women in light garments, children barefoot and quick as they darted between stalls.
James nodded toward a cluster of tents. “I’ll find out if they have a free place.”
While James disappeared into the crowd of traders, Aaliyah and Rowena watched the vibrant life of the Sanctuary Oasis.
For Aaliyah, this was not a new place — she had been here many times before. She was drawn to the lively marketplace nestled among the rocks, where everything from fragrant resins to strange, half-magical animals was sold. The scent of date palms and spices was familiar to her, and the hum of the market could calm her even after long days in the desert.
Suddenly, a man in a blue turban approached them.
He had sharp dark-brown eyes and a steady yet friendly voice. “Aaliyah? I haven’t seen you here in a long time.”
Rowena looked at him with curiosity but said nothing. She merely tilted her head slightly to study their faces, allowing Aaliyah to respond.
Aaliyah nodded faintly. “Yes, I’ve been away. For a long time. Longer than I originally intended.”
Her voice was steady, yet warm — carrying the weight of memories.
“But I never stopped thinking about the Sanctuary Oasis.”
She smiled, though more to herself than to anyone else.
“When I was a child, my father used to take me to the Spring of Knowledge,” she said quietly.
“He told me that water and memory become one there, and that some people can hear its voice.”
She paused for a moment, her gaze fixed on the lake shimmering silver between the palms.
“But we never heard it,” she added. “We only imagined it.”
She took a deep breath. “But fire… that one, yes. Its voice is fierce — sometimes angry, sometimes almost gentle. It never waits. It simply speaks — and demands to be heard.”
Rowena gave her a brief, thoughtful look but said nothing. She only adjusted her clothing.
“Fire never lies,” the man in the blue turban said seriously. “It tells only what people are afraid to say aloud. Just like Aaliyah.”
Then he smiled and gestured toward Rowena.
“I am Harim,” he introduced himself warmly. “It’s a pleasure to meet you.”
Aaliyah smiled lightly and added: “Harim is one of those who keep order in the oasis and help those who come here. You can trust him.”
Harim sat down on the ground beside the women, and together they waited for James to return. From time to time, his eyes scanned the surroundings — alert and watchful, yet calm.
When James finally appeared, he approached them and nodded to Harim.
“Come with me. I have a place for you,” Harim said decisively, leading them toward one of the larger tents at the edge of the oasis.
“You will stay here. It is a safe place, well guarded. You need to regain your strength.”
The women followed him, feeling the exhaustion of the desert heat and dust slowly leaving their bodies.
James walked closely behind them.
The three of them knew that while they were in the Sanctuary Oasis, they could rest — at least for a while.
