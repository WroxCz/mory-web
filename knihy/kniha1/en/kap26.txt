Chapter XXVI. - Confrontation
While she froze in shock, he merely straightened lazily, as if she had just interrupted him from quiet observation.
He held a glass of wine in his hand, standing by the bay window where shadows blended with the cold stone. He wore a loosely unbuttoned shirt, and in his eyes was that familiar, unpleasantly amused expression.
“Aaliyah,” he drawled her name with his typical smirk, a mixture of irony, laziness, and deliberate superiority. “The world really is small, isn’t it?”
Aaliyah stood rooted to the spot. Her heart pounded wildly. Her throat tightened, as if she had forgotten how to swallow.
Two years.
Two years without a word.
And now he stood there as if nothing had happened — and looked as though he were thoroughly entertained.
“I didn’t know you were here…”
Her voice sounded shakier than she wanted. Everything she had told herself over those months fell apart within seconds.
“Really?” he raised an eyebrow and took a sip from his glass. “And yet I’ve been here for quite some time.”
“Why did you leave without a word?” she asked quietly but directly. The pain she had tried so long to hide was clear in her voice.
William laughed — softly, maliciously. “Aaliyah, seriously? Are you playing the abandoned lover right now?”
He leaned a little closer, as if he wanted to swallow her in shadow.
“I thought we both understood each other back then. You were a distraction. A nice, willing, pleasant distraction. Long evenings in the desert are exhausting. You made them more bearable. Nothing more.”
His gaze slid over her face without a trace of tenderness.
“And now you want an explanation from me? For what?” he tilted his head. “For stopping the use of something that no longer entertained me?”
She felt her chin tremble, but she held herself together. She would not let him break her again. Not in front of him.
“You didn’t have to disappear.”
Her voice was quiet, but firm.
“I didn’t disappear,” he corrected coldly. “I simply left for something better. And honestly? I don’t understand why I should have explained it to you back then.”
His gaze was hard, merciless.
“But admit it — does it bother you more that I left you, or that I didn’t even look back?”
Aaliyah flushed. With humiliation. With anger.
William merely chuckled and turned his gaze back to the window.
“You should go. Castle corridors aren’t suitable for nostalgic outbursts. And besides… if you’re here with the people I think you are, someone might be missing you.”
The tone was soaked in double meaning.
Aaliyah said nothing. She turned on her heel and walked away quickly, because she felt confusion rising inside her — confusion she refused to relive.
Behind her, she heard his quiet laughter.
And she knew that was exactly what he wanted.

Aaliyah ran through the corridors like in a dream.
Her legs carried her on their own; she did not even know where she was going. Every step hurt — not physically, but inside, in places she had believed were long healed.
Two years.
Two years of silence.
And then it took only a few words, a single look from those arrogant eyes… and she felt like a girl again, begging to be loved.
William.
His name remained lodged in her heart, sharp as a shard of glass. Only moments ago he had looked at her with that same mocking smile with which he used to leave her waiting for hours. He spoke sweetly, smoothly — yet every word was poisonous. Every syllable sounded like ridicule, like a reminder that he could do whatever he wanted with her… and perhaps still could.
She stopped and leaned against the stone wall. Her fingers clenched the fabric of her cloak until her knuckles turned white.
I am stronger, she told herself silently. I am not the same anymore.
But even strong women have days when their voice trembles. When the past catches up with them.
She was shaking. With anger. With shame. With the shock that it could still hurt her.
But worse than his words was her own reaction.
She wanted to scream at him.
She wanted to hit him.
She wanted to tell him that he no longer mattered.
But instead, she stood there. Silent. And once again, he had the upper hand.
Tears began to fall from her eyes — quietly, without a single sob. She did not want to cry. Not because of him. But something inside her had broken.
Irreversibly.

James walked through the quiet corridors of the castle, where torchlight painted golden shadows on the stone walls.
It was not fear that drove him.
It was unease.
He stopped, leaned his shoulder against a pillar, and closed his eyes.
And in that very moment, he heard quiet, muffled sobs.
He turned — and saw her.
Aaliyah.
She stood at the end of the corridor, in a long cloak, without a hood. Her face was pale, her eyes red, her lips tightly pressed together, as if she refused to make a single sound. Her body trembled with restrained emotion.
“Aaliyah?” he called softly.
She flinched and tried to wipe the tears from her face with her palm and straighten herself.
“I’m sorry… I… I just needed some air.”
James said nothing. He simply walked slowly toward her and stopped right in front of her. He looked into her eyes — and saw everything.
The pain she had hidden for so long.
“You met him,” he said quietly.
She did not answer. She only nodded and closed her eyes.
“Was he the same as you remember?” he asked.
Aaliyah gave a bitter laugh.
“Worse. Two minutes and he had me in his grip again.”
James clenched his jaw.
Then he reached out — but instead of touching her, he let his hand fall back down.
“He doesn’t have you in his grip,” he said quietly. “Not anymore.”
“Because I see you. The way you are today.” He stepped closer. “As someone who has survived more than most — and is still standing.”
Aaliyah lowered her gaze.
“And what if I fall?”
“Then you’ll get back up. Or I’ll offer you my hand. I certainly won’t leave you lying there.”
The silence between them was different than before. Gentle.
At last, Aaliyah took a step toward him — small, almost imperceptible. But to James, it meant more than any words.
“Thank you,” she whispered. “For being here for me.”
He smiled.
“Always.”
After a while, Aaliyah calmed down. Her breathing slowed, as if she were slowly finding solid ground after the storm inside her.
She turned, intending to return to her room.
And at that very moment, she heard footsteps.
She lifted her head — and saw him.
James was walking behind her.
She stopped and allowed him to come closer. There was something unspoken in his eyes — a mixture of old pain, hope, and a quiet promise.
“Aaliyah,” he began softly, “I know what happened isn’t easy for you. But you’re not alone in this. I’m ready to stand by your side — if you allow me.”
And in that moment, Aaliyah finally admitted it to herself.
She wanted him to stand beside her.
“Thank you, James. Truly… I appreciate it,” she answered with a gentle smile. “I want you to stand by me.”
James froze for a moment.
He had not expected that. Not so directly. Not without hesitation, without doubt, without that familiar step back Aaliyah had always taken.
He looked at her more carefully, as if to make sure he had heard correctly. That this was not just a moment of weakness spoken in the fading pain after William.
“Are you sure?” he asked quietly. Not suspiciously. Carefully.
Aaliyah straightened. There was no shadow of hesitation left in her eyes.
“I…” she took a breath and then said it more firmly, leaving no room for doubt. “I’m not saying this because you happened to be there.”
She took a small step closer. This time without hesitation.
“I know you want me,” she said softly but clearly. “I see it. I’ve felt it for a long time.”
She lifted her eyes to his.
“And I want you.”
James froze for a moment.
“Aaliyah…” he breathed softly.
Then he slowly reached out his hand — this time without hesitation.
When Aaliyah placed her palm into his, he gently squeezed her hand.
Aaliyah took a deep breath, and together they continued down the corridor.
It felt like the beginning of something new — a path where they would no longer be alone.

Aaliyah and James walked side by side along the quiet corridor, their steps gradually falling into rhythm.
A silent understanding existed between them — one that needed no words. It was enough simply to be close.
Every step, every breath reminded them how strong the desire was to remain together despite everything.
They stopped by a narrow window, where moonlight brushed the stone walls and merged their silhouettes into a single image.
For a moment, they simply stood there, their hands intertwined, fingers reluctant to let go. Outside, the wind whispered among the stones, as if listening to their silence.
“I don’t know what awaits us,” Aaliyah said after a moment. Her voice was quieter than before, but stronger.
“But I don’t want to run anymore. Not from you. Not from myself.”
James leaned slowly toward her, as if drawn by something invisible. His gaze moved across her face, paused at her eyes, at her lips — and stayed there.
“Whatever comes… we’ll face it. Together,” he whispered. His breath mingled with hers. “You’re not alone. You never will be again.”
She closed her eyes and let that closeness sink deep inside, into places that had been frozen for years.
But now — now they were beginning to thaw.
James pulled back only far enough to see her again.
His hand rose and gently touched her face, brushing a strand of hair away from her forehead.
“I had so many questions,” he said in a quiet tone, “but right now, only one matters.”
Aaliyah tilted her head.
“Which one?”
“May I kiss you?”
For a single moment, a flicker of her old uncertainty appeared in her eyes.
But only for a second.
Then she nodded quietly.
And so he kissed her.
Gently.
It was not a kiss consumed by passion, but one filled with trust, tenderness, and quiet admiration.
When they pulled apart, they both stood there for a moment, simply breathing.
The air between them felt different.
As if the world had changed — or perhaps only they had.
James allowed himself a faint smile.
Then he drew a soft breath and looked at her.
“Come,” he said quietly. “I’ll walk you back.”
They walked slowly through the corridors, calm, shoulder to shoulder.
When they reached her door, he stopped. He did not step closer, nor back. He gave her space — just as he always did.
“Good night, Aaliyah,” he said calmly.
“Good night, James,” she replied with a gentle smile.
For a moment, their eyes met again.
Then Aaliyah stepped into the room and quietly closed the door behind her.
James remained standing in the corridor for a few seconds longer. Only then did he turn and walk away.
