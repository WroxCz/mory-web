Chapter XXVIII. – Infiltration
Beneath the dark sky, the Shadow encountered a group of knights patrolling the edge of the route to Stonegard. Its steps were unsteady, its gaze frightened. When they saw it, they immediately grew alert and surrounded it in a protective formation.
“Halt!” the captain called out. “Who are you, and what are you doing alone in the desert?”
The Shadow lifted eyes filled with feigned despair. Its voice trembled with fear — masterfully performed.
“I am a student of magic from the Tower of Pure Light… Please… our caravan was attacked. My master… the other healers… all dead. A man in black armor… he killed them.” It gasped for breath.
“I ran… I need help.”
The knights exchanged glances. Some reached for their weapons, others for their amulets.
“You spoke of a man in black armor carrying a spear and a sword?” the commander asked, clearly troubled.
“Yes… that demon… he killed them…” the Shadow trembled. “But if you hurry, you can grant peace to their souls. Or… find traces. Please.”
After a brief consultation, the unit split. Two of the knights, including the captain, mounted their horses with the Shadow.
“We will take you to Stonegard. The others will verify your words at the site of the attack.”
“Thank you,” the Shadow whispered. “Thank you… you have saved me.”

In the distance, beyond the crest of the dunes, Zarveth sat upon a raised cliff and silently watched as his bait rode away with the mounted company.
“Good hunt,” he murmured with satisfaction.
The sky above him darkened, as if the night itself approved of his success.
“The Shadow is inside,” he said quietly, almost ceremonially. “And soon… very soon, we will break the first seal.”
Then he rose and vanished without a trace into the darkness — as if he were nothing more than another whisper of the desert.
